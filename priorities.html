Practical Problems • Accountable Action

Ten People-First Priorities

These priorities define the work: listen to people, publish the facts, explain the tradeoffs, and build solutions that can be measured.

01

Lower the Cost of Living

Focus on housing, food, energy, transportation, healthcare, and the gap between wages and basic expenses.

02

Workforce Wages and Worker Power

Support fair pay, enforce wage laws, strengthen portable benefits, and make work pay enough to build a stable life.

03

Trades, Apprenticeships, and STEM

Expand paid training, technical education, union and nonunion pathways, and direct routes into high-demand careers.

04

Reliable Infrastructure

Prioritize water, power, roads, broadband, public transit, and disaster-resilient systems with transparent project tracking.

05

Accessible Healthcare and Mental Health

Improve access, affordability, prevention, crisis care, treatment capacity, and accountability for outcomes.

06

Veterans First

Reduce benefit delays, strengthen housing and mental-health support, and make the transition to civilian life easier.

07

Small-Business Relief

Simplify compliance, improve access to capital and technical help, and protect local businesses from unfair concentration.

08

Education for Real Life

Teach civics, personal finance, technology, trades, media literacy, and practical skills alongside core academics.

09

Government Transparency

Publish plain-language spending, votes, conflicts, schedules, project status, and corrections so the public can inspect the work.

10

One Team, Not Permanent Division

Work across political labels where goals overlap, disclose disagreements honestly, and judge proposals by results.
